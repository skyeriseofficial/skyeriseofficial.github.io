My Story

I'm Skye Energy Gibbs, and I know what it's like to be a gymnast under pressure. I started gymnastics at age 6 and trained hard every day. I trained at Sugar Land Gymnastics and later World Champions Centre under Coach Cecile & Coach Laurent (Simone Biles' coaches), competing through Level 8 and training up to Level 9.

Gymnastics was mentally and emotionally brutal. I was pushed before I was ready, faced fear of skills that kept me up at night, dealt with injuries, and carried extreme pressure from coaches and competitions. For years, my identity was completely wrapped up in gymnastics. I felt like I was nothing outside of it. If I had someone there who truly got it, who encouraged me and gave me the emotional and mental tools I needed, I would've been so much stronger. That's what I want to be for your gymnast.

The truth is: a gymnast can have all the talent in the world, but without a safe outlet or support, her potential can fade, and her love for the sport can disappear.

Sometimes you just need someone outside the gym who's been there and done that. That's why I'm here to be that person for your gymnast.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I know if my gymnast needs this?
If your gymnast is experiencing fear of skills, loss of motivation, anxiety about competitions, pressure from coaches, or seems to be losing their love for the sport, coaching can help. Even gymnasts who seem "fine" benefit from having a safe space to process the mental and emotional demands of elite training.
What happens in a typical session?
Sessions are personalized to your gymnast's needs. We might work on visualization techniques for scary skills, process feelings about coaching or competition pressure, practice confidence-building exercises, or simply create a space where your gymnast can vent and be heard. Every session is judgment-free and focused on what matters most to them.
Do you work with parents too?
Yes! The free 15-minute consultation is specifically for parents to discuss their gymnast's challenges and goals. I can also provide guidance to parents on how to best support their gymnast outside of our sessions. However, the coaching sessions themselves are one-on-one with the gymnast to create a safe, confidential space.
What ages do you work with?
I work with gymnasts ages 6 to 17. My approach adapts to each age group. Younger gymnasts might focus more on building confidence and managing fear, while older athletes often work on deeper identity issues, burnout prevention, and preparing for life beyond gymnastics.
How quickly will we see results?
Every gymnast is different. Some see immediate relief after just one session of being heard and validated. Others need several sessions to work through deeper mental blocks or rebuild motivation. Consistency is key. Gymnasts who commit to regular sessions (weekly or bi-weekly) typically see the most significant and lasting changes.
How do sessions work logistically?
All sessions are conducted via Google Meet, so your gymnast can connect from anywhere with an internet connection. Sessions are scheduled at times that work for your family's busy gymnastics schedule. Flexible scheduling available.
Are you a licensed therapist?
I am not a licensed therapist. I'm a former elite gymnast who understands the unique mental and emotional challenges of the sport firsthand. I provide mentorship, mindset coaching, and a safe space for gymnasts to process their experiences. If your gymnast is dealing with severe anxiety, depression, or trauma, I recommend working with a licensed mental health professional in addition to or instead of coaching.
What if my gymnast is shy or hesitant?
That's completely normal! Many gymnasts are nervous about opening up at first. I create a comfortable, judgment-free environment where they can share at their own pace. Sometimes just knowing someone "gets it" because they've lived it makes all the difference. The free consultation also helps ease any nervousness.
